What is Facilities Maintenance Work?

Commercial service work for buildings and properties. This is ongoing electrical maintenance, repairs, and emergency service for commercial properties, NOT one-time construction projects.

Types of Facilities Maintenance Work:

  • Emergency Service Calls: Power outages, electrical failures, safety hazards
  • Preventive Maintenance: Regular inspections, testing, scheduled maintenance
  • Repair Work: Fixing broken panels, outlets, lighting, circuits
  • Code Compliance: Bringing systems up to code, passing inspections
  • Tenant Service: Electrical work for individual tenants in buildings
  • Upgrades: Panel replacements, lighting retrofits, system improvements
Key Difference: Facilities maintenance is ONGOING SERVICE, not project-based construction. You're building long-term relationships and recurring revenue, not bidding one-off jobs.

Discovery Questions (Ask These First)

Don't pitch first. Ask questions to understand their situation:

  • "How many properties do you manage?" (understand scope)
  • "What's your biggest electrical headache right now?" (find pain point)
  • "How do you currently handle electrical emergencies?" (understand current solution)
  • "How often do electrical issues come up?" (understand frequency)
  • "Do you have a regular electrician or call different people each time?" (understand relationship status)
  • "What's most important to you in an electrical contractor?" (understand priorities)
  • "Are you required to have preventive maintenance programs for insurance or compliance?" (NFPA 70B)
  • "How old are the electrical systems in your buildings?" (assess needs)
  • "What's your process for approving new vendors?" (understand decision process)

The NFPA 70B Compliance Opportunity

üö® NEW REQUIREMENT (2023): NFPA 70B Now MANDATORY

What changed: As of 2023, NFPA 70B requires documented electrical maintenance plans for commercial buildings.

Why it matters: Insurance companies are now checking compliance. Buildings without maintenance plans risk coverage issues.

The opportunity: Most property managers don't know about this yet. YOU can educate them and provide the solution.

How to Use This in Your Pitch:

"Are you aware of the new NFPA 70B requirements that went into effect in 2023? It now REQUIRES documented electrical maintenance plans for commercial buildings. Insurance companies are starting to check compliance. Properties without documented maintenance programs could face coverage issues. Our preventive maintenance contracts ensure you're compliant and have all the documentation you need. We handle inspections, testing, maintenance, and provide detailed reports that satisfy NFPA 70B requirements. Would it make sense to discuss a maintenance program for your properties?"

Key Stats to Mention:

  • Facilities WITHOUT preventive maintenance have 3x more electrical equipment damage
  • Emergency electrical repairs cost 2-4x more than preventive maintenance
  • Average unplanned electrical downtime costs $5,000-$50,000+ depending on facility
