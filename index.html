Discovery Questions (Ask These)

Don't just pitch. Ask questions to understand their needs:

  • "What types of projects do you typically work on?" (understand their business)
  • "How many electrical subcontractors do you currently use?" (understand competition)
  • "What's most important to you when selecting an electrical sub?" (understand priorities)
  • "What challenges have you had with electrical contractors in the past?" (understand pain points)
  • "What's your process for adding contractors to your bid list?" (understand next steps)
  • "Do you have any upcoming projects where electrical is a significant scope?" (understand timing)

Common Objections & How to Handle Them

❌ "We already have electrical contractors we use"

✓ Response: "That's great you have contractors you trust. Many of our best clients came to us initially as a backup resource, for when your main contractor is at capacity, emergencies when they're unavailable, or specialty projects. Would it make sense to get prequalified as a backup option?"

❌ "You're too small"

✓ Response: "Our size is actually an advantage. You get direct access to the owner for fast decisions, personal attention on every project, and we're not juggling hundreds of jobs. Plus, our triple licensing (C-10, B, A) gives us capabilities that larger contractors often don't have."

❌ "Send me your information"

✓ Response: "Absolutely, I'll send over our company overview, licenses, and references. While I have you though, what types of projects are you typically working on? That helps me send the most relevant information." (Then ask discovery questions)

❌ "We need someone who can handle large projects"

✓ Response: "Our B and A licenses actually qualify us for larger projects that C-10-only contractors can't touch. We've worked on projects ranging from small service calls to full commercial buildings. What size projects are we talking about?"

❌ "I'm not the right person to talk to"

✓ Response: "No problem, who would be the best person to speak with about electrical subcontractors? [Get name] Would you mind if I mentioned you referred me when I reach out?" (Get warm intro)

Where to Find Prospects

1. Job Site Visits

Best approach: Visit active commercial construction sites in LA

  • Look for GC signs on site
  • Find superintendent or project manager
  • Brief introduction, leave card
  • Follow up by email within 24 hours

2. Industry Events

  • AGC (Associated General Contractors) LA chapter meetings
  • ABC (Associated Builders and Contractors) events
  • Construction networking events
  • Introduce yourself to 5+ people at each event

3. Leverage Current Clients

  • Ask Stratus, PVC, Brite Construction for referrals
  • Request introductions to other GCs they know
  • Use them as warm references

4. Cold Outreach

  • Research active GCs in LA area
  • Find decision makers (estimators, PMs, ops managers)

Follow-Up Strategy

Critical Stat: 78% of leads go to the first contractor who responds. Speed matters!

After Initial Meeting

  • Within 24 hours: Send thank you email with company info
  • Day 3: Email to confirm they received materials
  • Week 1: Phone call to answer questions
  • Week 2: Email with relevant info (project you completed, article, etc.)
  • Month 1: Check-in call
  • Ongoing: Monthly touchpoint (email or call)

What to Track

  • Company name and contact info
  • Date of contact
  • Who you spoke with
  • What you discussed
  • Next steps and follow-up date
  • Any specific needs or upcoming projects

Key Reminders

  • Listen more than you talk: Ask questions, understand their needs before pitching
  • Speed matters: Respond to inquiries within hours, not days
  • Build relationships: Think long-term partnerships, not one-time transactions
  • Use our differentiators: Triple licensing, small/nimble, LA expertise, proven clients
  • Be professional: Every interaction represents Shaffer Construction
  • Follow up systematically: Most deals happen after multiple touches
  • Ask for referrals: When you meet good people, ask who else they know
  • Stay positive: Rejection is normal, keep going
